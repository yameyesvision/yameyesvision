# Phase 3: Video Scripts
## To the Son Who Saved Me | Adam Llamas

*15 short-form scripts — 30 to 60 seconds each*
*Written to sound like real talk, not content*
*Each script includes: setup, hook, middle, closing line, and direction notes*

---

### SCRIPT 01 — Why I Wrote This Book
**Best for:** Launch video, pinned post, bio link
**Tone:** Quiet, direct, personal
**Length:** ~45 seconds

---

**[Direction: Sit down. No ring light perfection. Natural light. Look at the camera like you're talking to one person.]**

"I wrote this book because I wanted to leave my son something that says all I couldn't really get the chance to say.

The lessons. The truth. The things you only learn by living through them.

I didn't write it because I had everything figured out.

I wrote it because I didn't.

And I needed him to know — regardless of what happened, regardless of distance or time or circumstances —

I was always there. Every decision I made had his name tucked inside it.

This book is called *To the Son Who Saved Me.*

Written for one.

Speaks to many."

**[Hold. Don't rush the end. Let it sit.]**

---

### SCRIPT 02 — The Froot Loops Story
**Best for:** First viral-potential video — lead with this one early
**Tone:** Warm, storytelling, quiet payoff
**Length:** ~55 seconds

---

**[Direction: Relaxed. Slight smile at the end. Don't perform it — just tell it.]**

"For almost the first year and a half of knowing my son — he didn't know I was his dad.

I wasn't gonna rush it. I wanted it to be real. Organic. His.

So I just showed up. Every time.

Then one day — I was joking with him about getting Apple Jacks when I knew he wanted Froot Loops.

He looked at me dead serious. Put the iPad down.

And said — *'Dad. I don't want Apple Jacks. Make me some Froot Loops.'*

He probably said it like it was the most natural thing in the world.

For me — it was everything.

In five years, I've heard it maybe fifteen times.

I've never pushed for it.

Every single time it comes out — it puts a smile on my heart that nothing else can match.

Because it was real.

It came from him.

Say the true things. Let them be real. And when they come —

let them mean everything they're supposed to mean."

### SCRIPT 03 — One-Way Ticket
**Best for:** Reels / TikTok — strong hook, builds fast
**Tone:** Bold, honest, a little raw
**Length:** ~50 seconds

---

**[Direction: Standing. Energy in your body. This one moves.]**

"I bought a one-way ticket to Thailand.

No plan. No contacts. Nobody waiting on the other end.

Just a gut feeling and a name I kept in my chest everywhere I went.

The day I flew out — I recorded a video. Said I was ready for change. Ready for growth.

Then I told the truth.

*I'm nervous as hell. I'm scared too.*

I posted a caption that said — 'Your new life is going to cost you your old one.'

Behind that post — I genuinely thought I might be losing my mind.

But I got on the plane anyway.

That's courage.

Not the absence of fear.

Movement — despite it."

**[Pause. Let that last line land.]**

### SCRIPT 04 — The 14-Hour Drive
**Best for:** Film in the car on an actual pickup trip — raw, real
**Tone:** Grounded, proud, father energy
**Length:** ~45 seconds

---

**[Direction: Film yourself driving. Or parked before you pull off. Real environment.]**

"Every time I go pick up my son — it's a 14-hour round trip.

Door to door. By myself. Every time.

Most people hear that and say — that's too far.

I hear that and think — not far enough.

Because those drives became something.

We taught each other more on those drives than anywhere else.

He learned to tell time on one of them.

On another one — Marc Anthony came on and neither of us could stop ourselves.

We just rode. Talked. Laughed. Sang.

Mile by mile — that's where the bond was built.

Love isn't loud.

It's consistent."

### SCRIPT 05 — What the Block Taught Me
**Best for:** Cultural/nostalgia hook — good for new audience reach
**Tone:** Reflective, grounded, proud of where you came from
**Length:** ~50 seconds

---

**[Direction: Talking head. Calm. This one is about legacy before you knew what legacy was.]**

"I grew up on the Lower East Side of New York in the nineties.

And yeah — I saw what broken promises did to people.

Family stealing from family. Friends disappearing when things got real.

But I also saw the rare few who kept their word no matter what.

My uncle — on a block full of chaos — that man was at the bus stop every morning at 8am.

Feet crossed. Waiting.

Rain. Snow. Pounding heat. Didn't matter.

I didn't fully understand why it stuck with me back then.

I just knew — I respected it.

Those men became a blueprint I didn't even know I was building from.

Your reputation walks into the room before you do.

What's yours saying?"

### SCRIPT 06 — Gratitude in the Hard Years
**Best for:** Morning-energy content / motivational without being fake
**Tone:** Real, not preachy — this happened to you, you're not teaching
**Length:** ~45 seconds

---

**[Direction: Morning setting if possible. Low key. Coffee in hand is fine.]**

"In 2022 I was in Miami rebuilding my life.

New city. Thin margins. More questions than answers.

Every time I got in my car — I listed five things I was grateful for.

Family. Friends. My health. Life.

And him. Always him. Always at the top.

Some days I'd say his name and just sit with it for a second.

Some days — that was the only thing that put the car in drive.

Not a plan. Not a strategy.

Just his name. Just knowing I was his dad.

That was enough.

Gratitude doesn't change what you have.

It changes how you see — and who you become."

### SCRIPT 07 — The Call I Almost Didn't Make
**Best for:** Emotional impact — check on your people content
**Tone:** Serious. No music on this one. Let it breathe.
**Length:** ~55 seconds

---

**[Direction: Direct to camera. Still. This is important.]**

"Something told me to check on a friend.

I hadn't talked to him in a while. No reason. Just life.

But something said — pick up the phone.

I almost didn't.

Almost said — he's probably fine. I'll get to it later.

I listened to that feeling. I dialed.

What I found on the other end stopped me cold.

He was in a dark place.

A place where he was thinking about not being here anymore.

That call mattered more than either of us could have known.

And I think about it all the time.

What if I said *later*?

Later isn't promised.

Don't let doubt or busyness talk you out of reaching out.

Send the message. Make the call.

Your words might be the reason someone decides to stay."

**[No CTA needed. Let it end there.]**

### SCRIPT 08 — Pride
**Best for:** Men's content — accountability, growth
**Tone:** Direct, no softening, been there
**Length:** ~40 seconds

---

**[Direction: Straight to camera. Short and sharp.]**

"Pride almost cost me everything.

Good people. Real relationships. Moments I couldn't get back.

I burned bridges — out of ego. Out of fear. Out of being too stubborn to say two words.

And every time I told myself I was justified.

The growth came when I swallowed it.

When I picked up the phone first.

When I said — I was wrong.

Every single time I did that — I walked away lighter.

Don't let ego be the reason you lose good people.

Don't let pride build walls where bridges should be.

Be the one who reaches back first.

That's not weakness. That's leadership."

### SCRIPT 09 — Reading from the Book
**Best for:** Pinned post or launch week — most powerful script in this set
**Tone:** Still. Present. Just your voice and the words.
**Length:** ~60 seconds

---

**[Direction: Hold the book or set it down in front of you. Read slowly. Don't perform it. This should feel like a private moment the viewer got to witness.]**

"I want to read you something I wrote to my son.

*'If you're reading this, it means life gave us time. Or maybe it didn't. Either way, I needed you to have this.*

*I've written a lot of things in my life. But nothing has ever felt more important than this. It's not perfect. Neither am I. But every word is yours — straight from my heart.*

*You were the light. From the moment I held you, I knew my life had changed. I didn't just become a father. I became a man with purpose.'*

That's the opening.

This book is called *To the Son Who Saved Me.*

If that hit you — it was written for you too.

Link in my bio."

### SCRIPT 10 — Legacy
**Best for:** Shareable, emotional — good closer video
**Tone:** Slow, intentional, spoken word energy
**Length:** ~50 seconds

---

**[Direction: Speak slowly on this one. Pause between each thought. Let the words land before moving to the next one.]**

"One day your name is going to be spoken — after you're gone.

And it won't be your titles they remember.

It won't be your possessions.

It'll be how you made people feel.

The phone call you made that kept someone here.

The open door you gave when you didn't have to.

The consistency — the quiet, unglamorous, nobody-watching consistency.

That's legacy.

Not what you kept.

What you passed down.

Flames flicker and fade.

Fire spreads. Warms. Sustains.

Pass on the fire.

Both hands. Every time."

### SCRIPT 11 — The Great-Grandmother
**Best for:** Emotional storytelling — unexpected, hits different
**Tone:** Warm, slow, real
**Length:** ~55 seconds

---

**[Direction: Softer energy. This one is about love in unexpected places.]**

"When I walked through her door — she cried for five straight minutes.

Not quiet tears.

The kind that come from years of holding things in.

Pain. Loneliness. Relief — all at once.

She's my great-grandmother on my stepdad's side.

We weren't even that close before.

But something told me to call. To show up.

I stayed.

What started as me showing up for her — slowly turned into something I needed just as much.

She became my best friend.

She turned ninety this past October.

Still sharp. Still strong.

And every time I'd bring my son back from one of our drives — her whole face lit up when she heard his footsteps.

That joy was worth every mile.

Sometimes the right choice isn't the easiest one.

But it's the one that shapes you."

### SCRIPT 13 — Your Word
**Best for:** Integrity / character content — men's audience
**Tone:** Grounded, strong, lived in
**Length:** ~45 seconds

**[Direction: Direct. Confident but not loud. This one is about character.]**

Just your promise.

### SCRIPT 14 — Rebuilding Yourself
**Best for:** Personal growth / men rebuilding — wide reach
**Tone:** Honest, no inspiration-speak, this is lived experience
**Length:** ~50 seconds

---

**[Direction: Real setting. Relatable energy. This should feel like a conversation, not a speech.]**

"I changed the way I talked to myself.

I changed my habits.

I changed the people I allowed into my space.

None of that happened overnight.

It happened choice by choice.

And there were plenty of days I went backwards before I went forward.

But here's what I know now that I didn't know then —

Every sacrifice. Every lonely night. Every uncomfortable truth I made myself face.

It was all worth it.

Not because it led to some perfect version of myself.

But because it led me back to me.

You're not trying to become someone new.

You're trying to find out who you actually are — underneath everything you've been performing.

That's the work.

And it's worth doing."

## FILMING NOTES

**The ones to film first (priority order):**

1. Script 09 — Reading from the book *(most powerful, best for launch)*
2. Script 01 — Why I wrote this book *(your foundation video)*
3. Script 02 — The Froot Loops story *(best viral potential)*
4. Script 15 — The camping photo *(shortest, strongest visual)*
5. Script 07 — The call I almost didn't make *(emotional reach)*

**General filming tips:**
- Natural light over ring light when possible — it matches the book's tone
- You don't need to be standing or "on" — some of the best ones can be filmed sitting at a table, in the car, outside
- Don't re-record until it sounds perfect — the slight imperfection is the point
- If you stumble on a word, pause and keep going — don't start over. That realness is what makes people stay
- No heavy background music on the emotional scripts (07, 09, 11, 12) — silence or very low ambient sound only
- Scripts 03, 05, 08, 13 can carry more energy and music underneath
