# Phase 5: Review System
## To the Son Who Saved Me | Adam Llamas

*How to generate reviews without it feeling transactional.*
*Messages, instructions, and thank-you responses — built around appreciation, not ask.*

---

## WHY REVIEWS MATTER (AND WHY MOST AUTHORS ASK WRONG)

Amazon reviews are the single most important lever for an indie author.

Not because they make you look good — because they make the book discoverable.
Amazon's algorithm surfaces books with reviews. More reviews = more strangers finding it.
Every review is essentially free advertising that lives forever.

But here's where most authors get it wrong:
They ask too fast. Too formally. Too transactionally.

*"Please leave a review, it really helps!"* feels like a favor request.
It puts the reader in an uncomfortable position.

The right way: make it feel like an invitation, not an obligation.
You're giving them a chance to be part of something — not doing you a favor.

## PART 1: MESSAGE TO SEND AFTER SOMEONE BUYS OR READS

*Use this 5–7 days after someone buys, or once they've had time to read.*
*Send via DM, text, or email depending on how you know them.*

---

### Message 01 — For Someone You Know Personally

> Hey [Name] — just wanted to check in and see if you had a chance to sit with the book.
>
> No rush — I know life moves fast. But if anything hit home, I'd genuinely love to hear about it. Not for any reason other than I put a lot into that, and hearing what lands for people means something to me.
>
> And if you feel moved to leave a review on Amazon — even a sentence or two — it makes a real difference in helping other people find it. Indie publishing lives and dies by word of mouth, and a review from a real reader carries more weight than any ad I could run.
>
> Either way — thank you for picking it up. That meant a lot.

---

### Message 02 — For Someone Who Reached Out After Reading

> That message you sent — I've thought about it more than once since you sent it.
>
> This is exactly why I wrote this. Not for sales. For moments like that.
>
> If you're open to it — would you be willing to share that on Amazon as a review? Just what you told me. Your honest words. It doesn't need to be long or formal. A sentence or two from a real reader does more for this book than anything else.
>
> Here's the direct link to leave one: [Amazon review link]
>
> Thank you for being part of this.

---

### Message 03 — For a Stranger / New Follower Who Bought

> Hey [Name] — genuinely appreciate you picking up the book. Means a lot as an indie author.
>
> When you get through it — if anything resonated, I'd be honored if you left a quick review on Amazon. It doesn't need to be a full write-up. Even a line or two about what hit home helps more people find it.
>
> Here's the link: [Amazon review link]
>
> Hope it's worth every page.

---

### Message 04 — For a Podcaster or Influencer Who Received a Free Copy

> Hey [Name] — just wanted to follow up and see if you had a chance to get into the book.
>
> No pressure on anything — I sent it because I thought it would resonate with you personally, not to check a box.
>
> If you do read it and feel moved to share anything — a post, a review on Amazon, even a DM about what hit home — I'd be grateful. But only if it's genuine. Forced endorsements help nobody.
>
> Either way, thank you for your time. I know how much of it gets pulled in different directions.

## PART 2: SIMPLE INSTRUCTIONS FOR LEAVING A REVIEW

*Include these instructions whenever you send a review request.*
*Make it easy — most people want to help but don't know how.*

---

### Instructions (Short Version — for DM)

> Here's how to leave one in under two minutes:
> 1. Go to the book on Amazon: [link]
> 2. Scroll down to the reviews section
> 3. Click "Write a customer review"
> 4. Give it a star rating and write a sentence or two — whatever felt true for you
> 5. Hit submit
>
> That's it. No account needed beyond a standard Amazon login.

---

### Instructions (Longer Version — for email)

> **How to leave a review (takes about 2 minutes):**
>
> 1. Click this link: [Amazon book page link]
> 2. Scroll down until you see "Customer Reviews"
> 3. Click the button that says "Write a customer review"
> 4. Choose your star rating (1–5 stars)
> 5. In the text box — write whatever felt true. One sentence is enough. Three sentences is plenty.
> 6. Click "Submit"
>
> **You don't need to:**
> - Write a formal review
> - Summarize the book
> - Be a writer
>
> **Just answer one of these if you're not sure what to say:**
> - What part of the book stayed with you?
> - Who would you recommend this to?
> - How did it make you feel?
>
> Your honest words — even one line — are worth more than a hundred ad impressions.

## PART 3: THANK-YOU RESPONSES FOR REVIEWERS

*When someone leaves a review — always respond. Always.*
*These are for DMs, comments, or emails after someone tells you they left one.*

---

### Thank-You 01 — Short and Genuine

> I saw it. Thank you.
>
> You didn't have to do that — and you did. That means more than I can put into words right now.
>
> Genuinely grateful for you.

---

### Thank-You 02 — When the Review Was Emotional / Personal

> I read your review three times.
>
> That's what this was written for. Not the stars — the words. The fact that it landed the way it was meant to land.
>
> Thank you for taking the time to put that out there. You just helped the next person find this who needs it.
>
> I won't forget that.

---

### Thank-You 03 — When a Stranger Leaves a Review

> I don't know you — but I want you to know that I saw this.
>
> An honest review from a real reader means everything to an indie author. You didn't have to do that.
>
> Thank you for being part of this. Really.

---

### Thank-You 04 — When a Podcaster or Influencer Shares or Reviews

> You didn't owe me that. And you did it anyway.
>
> That's the kind of thing that stays with a person. Thank you — genuinely.
>
> If there's ever anything I can do to show up for what you're building — just say the word.

---

### Thank-You 05 — Public Response (if they post the review publicly on social)

> This stopped me mid-scroll.
>
> Thank you for putting this out there. You just put this book in front of someone who needed it — and you didn't even know it.
>
> This is exactly why I wrote it.

## REVIEW SYSTEM CALENDAR

*A simple timeline for the first 90 days after launch.*

---

**Week 1 — Seed Reviews**
Reach out to 10–15 people who already know you and the story.
Friends, family, anyone who was around for any chapter of it.
Ask personally — text or DM, not a mass blast.
Goal: 5–10 reviews before you push hard on promotion.

**Week 2–4 — Warm Audience**
Anyone who comments on your content, DMs you, or buys through your link.
Send Message 01 or 03 within 5–7 days of purchase.
Goal: 15–25 reviews total.

**Month 2 — Content-Driven**
As your videos and posts gain traction, new readers will come in.
Add the review CTA to the bottom of your email list messages.
Include a soft mention in one video per week.
Goal: 35–50 reviews total.

**Month 3 — Social Proof Push**
Screenshot and share positive reviews (with permission) on your social platforms.
Reviews become content. Content drives more reviews.
Goal: 50+ reviews — this is the threshold where Amazon starts surfacing the book organically.

---

## ONE RULE

Never incentivize a review.

Don't offer anything in exchange — not a free copy specifically for a review, not a shoutout, nothing.
Amazon's terms prohibit it, and more importantly — it compromises the authenticity of the review.

The ask is simple: *if it meant something to you, say so.*
That's it.
