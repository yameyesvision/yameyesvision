### Full Description

Some books are written to be read. This one was written to be felt.

Before Ethan could read, his father was already writing this for him.

*To the Son Who Saved Me: 10 Truths for the Road Ahead* is a raw, deeply personal letter from a father to his son — part memoir, part life manual, all heart. It's the book that gets written when a man survives himself and wants to make sure his child doesn't have to start from zero.

Growing up on the Lower East Side of New York in the nineties, Adam Llamas found himself raising a family while still being raised by the streets. He hustled his way to fifteen years behind the turntables, building a name, a career, and a lifestyle that looked like success from the outside. But underneath it all, he was carrying weight he hadn't dealt with — and eventually, life made him deal with it.

What followed was a decade of real reckoning: walking away from music, moving across state lines, flying solo to Thailand on a one-way ticket, rebuilding himself from scratch, and discovering that the boy who called him "Dad" for the first time over a bowl of Froot Loops had already saved his life without knowing it.

This book holds the ten truths he earned the hard way — not rules, not a manual, but hard-won wisdom from a man who has lived it, bled through it, and found his way forward. Lessons about gratitude and grief. About keeping your word even when it's inconvenient. About asking for help when pride says don't. About what love looks like when you actually mean it. About falling apart with dignity and rising back up with purpose.

Written for Ethan. But honest enough to reach anyone who has ever been lost, rebuilt themselves in silence, or loved someone more than they knew how to say.

**This is a father's promise, written in full.**

### Full Bio

Adam Llamas grew up on the Lower East Side of New York City in the nineties — raised primarily by his grandmother, shaped by a block that had its own rules, and guided by a handful of people who chose to pass something real down to him before he even knew he needed it.

He found his way to music early. By nineteen, he was building his name as a mixtape DJ out of a barbershop basement in the neighborhood. What started as a dream became fifteen years behind the turntables — close to 200,000 CDs distributed, stages and clubs across the country, a name that traveled further than he expected.

But music was always the outside story. The inside one was harder and more honest. Carrying weight from a childhood without a biological father. Navigating the streets without always making the right calls. Learning what integrity meant by watching men who had it — and losing respect for men who didn't. Falling in love. Falling apart. Starting over.

He walked away from music when the rooms stopped feeling right. Moved to Florida. Found out what it cost to show up for someone every single day — fourteen hours round trip, weekend after weekend, because that's what love looks like when it's consistent.

He boarded a one-way flight to Thailand with no plan and not enough money — just a gut feeling and a name he kept in his chest everywhere he went. He spent a year there doing the real work: facing himself, reconnecting with purpose, and deciding what kind of man he actually wanted to be.

Then he came home, sat down, and wrote this book.

Adam currently lives in Florida. He is a father, a writer, a runner, and a work in progress.

*To the Son Who Saved Me* is his first book.

### About the Book (Press Version)

*To the Son Who Saved Me: 10 Truths for the Road Ahead* is the debut book by Adam Llamas, a father and former DJ from New York City's Lower East Side. Part memoir, part life manual, the book is structured around ten truths earned through real experience — covering gratitude, integrity, vulnerability, legacy, and resilience.

The book draws from Llamas's own journey: growing up without a biological father, building a fifteen-year career as a mixtape DJ, walking away from that life, living abroad in Thailand, and ultimately discovering that fatherhood was the thing that saved him.

*To the Son Who Saved Me* speaks to fathers, sons, and anyone who has ever rebuilt themselves in silence and needed permission to say so out loud.
