# To the Son Who Saved Me — Book Description

## Amazon / Back Cover Description

---

**Some books are written to be read. This one was written to be felt.**

Before Ethan could read, his father was already writing this for him.

*To the Son Who Saved Me: 10 Truths for the Road Ahead* is a raw, deeply personal letter from a father to his son — part memoir, part life manual, all heart. It's the book that gets written when a man survives himself and wants to make sure his child doesn't have to start from zero.

Growing up on the Lower East Side of New York in the nineties, the author found himself raising a family while still being raised by the streets. He hustled his way to fifteen years behind the turntables, building a name, a career, and a lifestyle that looked like success from the outside. But underneath it all, he was carrying weight he hadn't dealt with — and eventually, life made him deal with it.

What followed was a decade of real reckoning: walking away from music, moving across state lines, flying solo to Thailand on a one-way ticket, rebuilding himself from scratch, and discovering that the boy who called him "Dad" for the first time over a bowl of Froot Loops had already saved his life without knowing it.

This book holds the ten truths he earned the hard way — not rules, not a manual, but hard-won wisdom from a man who has lived it, bled through it, and found his way forward. Lessons about gratitude and grief. About keeping your word even when it's inconvenient. About asking for help when pride says don't. About what love looks like when you actually mean it. About falling apart with dignity and rising back up with purpose.

Written for Ethan. But honest enough to reach anyone who has ever been lost, rebuilt themselves in silence, or loved someone more than they knew how to say.

**This is a father's promise, written in full.**

## Goodreads Description

*To the Son Who Saved Me* started as a letter a father needed his son to have — just in case life didn't give them enough time.

It became something much bigger.

This is the story of a man from the Lower East Side of New York who spent fifteen years building a name as a mixtape DJ, then spent the next decade quietly rebuilding himself. Walking away from the streets. Walking away from music. Moving across state lines. Boarding a one-way flight to Thailand with no plan and $not enough — just a gut feeling and the memory of a little boy who called him "Dad" for the first time over a bowl of Froot Loops.

Structured around ten truths earned through real living — not theory, not inspiration quotes — this book covers:

- **Gratitude** as a daily practice that held him together when nothing else did
- **Words** and why love left unspoken becomes regret
- **Integrity** and what it means to let your yes mean yes
- **Accountability** and the courage it takes to say *I was wrong*
- **Curiosity** as the force that saved his life more than once
- **Asking for help** when pride says don't
- **Giving** without keeping score
- **Legacy** — passing on fire, not just a flame
- **Resilience** — falling with grace and rising with grit
- **Purpose** — what it means to live a life that was actually yours

Part memoir. Part manifesto. Part love letter.

Written for Ethan. Honest enough for everyone.
