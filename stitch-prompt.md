Build me a complete, multi-page book promotion website for "To the Son Who Saved Me: 10 Truths for the Road Ahead" by author Adam Llamas. This is a deeply personal memoir and life manual written as a father's letter to his son. The design must feel honest, warm, and grounded — like the book itself. Not flashy. Not corporate. Real.

## DESIGN SYSTEM

### Color Palette

- **Primary Background:** #FAF7F2 (warm cream — used for most page backgrounds)
- **Dark Background:** #1A2E27 (deep forest green — used for full-width quote sections and footer)
- **Secondary Background:** #F0E8DC (warm linen — used for alternating sections)
- **Primary Text:** #1C1C1C (near black — all body copy)
- **Heading Text:** #1A2E27 (deep green — all headlines)
- **Accent / CTA Color:** #C4862A (burnt gold — buttons, highlights, numbered list accents)
- **Accent Hover:** #A36E1F (darker gold — button hover state)
- **Muted Text:** #7A6A5A (warm brown-gray — subheadlines, captions, small text)
- **Divider / Border:** #E2D9CE (soft warm line — section dividers)
- **White:** #FFFFFF (used sparingly — inside dark sections)

### Typography

- **Display / Hero Headlines:** Playfair Display — Bold — size 56–72px — color #1A2E27
- **Section Headlines:** Playfair Display — SemiBold — size 36–44px — color #1A2E27
- **Subheadlines:** DM Sans — Medium — size 18–22px — color #7A6A5A — letter spacing 0.02em
- **Body Copy:** DM Sans — Regular — size 16–18px — line height 1.8 — color #1C1C1C
- **Pull Quotes:** Playfair Display — Italic — size 28–36px — color #FFFFFF (on dark background) or #1A2E27 (on light)
- **Button Text:** DM Sans — SemiBold — size 15px — uppercase — letter spacing 0.08em
- **Navigation:** DM Sans — Medium — size 14px — letter spacing 0.06em — uppercase

### Buttons

- **Primary CTA:** Background #C4862A — Text #FFFFFF — Padding 16px 36px — Border radius 4px — No drop shadow — Hover: #A36E1F
- **Secondary CTA:** Background transparent — Border 2px solid #C4862A — Text #C4862A — Same padding — Hover: fill with #C4862A, text white

### Overall Feeling

Warm but not soft. Quiet strength. Like a man handing something important to someone he loves. No gradients. No stock photo energy. Generous white space. Let the words breathe. Photography should feel candid and real — not posed. Every design decision should feel like it belongs to the story.

### SECTION 2 — FULL STORY

**Layout:** Two column. Left: body copy. Right: author photo (different from hero — natural, real). Background #FAF7F2. Padding 100px.

**Section label (#C4862A, DM Sans uppercase, 13px):**
HIS STORY

**Body copy (DM Sans, #1C1C1C, 17px, line-height 1.9):**

I grew up on the Lower East Side of New York City in the nineties. Raised mostly by my grandmother — a woman I loved deeply, who was fighting her own battles. My mom was around, doing her best. My brother got pulled into the streets early. My biological father was nowhere.

So I figured things out the way a lot of kids like me do — by watching, absorbing, and sometimes choosing wrong on purpose just to see what happened.

What I had, though, was people. My uncle, who suited up for work every single day at 8am at the bus stop — rain, snow, it didn't matter. My stepdad, who stepped into a space I didn't have a name for and gave it balance. Friends' parents who treated me like their own.

That's fire being passed, even from imperfect hands.

I spent fifteen years as a DJ. Built a name, distributed close to 200,000 CDs out of a basement barbershop on the Lower East Side, played stages across the country. From the outside, it looked like I had it together. On the inside, I was carrying weight I hadn't looked at yet.

Eventually I walked away from music. Moved to Florida. Made 14-hour round trips on weekends because that was what showing up looked like. Became the primary companion to a 90-year-old great-grandmother who cried for five minutes the day I walked through her door. Boarded a one-way flight to Thailand with no plan and not enough money — just a gut feeling and a name I kept in my chest everywhere I went.

I came back different. Not because I had everything figured out. Because I finally stopped pretending I did.

*To the Son Who Saved Me* is that. A letter to my son Ethan. Written so he'd always have me — even when I wasn't there.

I'm not a finished product. I'm writing this from the middle — a man still figuring things out, still showing up, still choosing.
